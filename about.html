About Me

I was born into a small family in 1996. I am the second son to a mechanic and a cleaner. Two years later my parents collaborated again and my sister was born. I grew up in small town in rural Queensland where the main industry is sugar cane, mangoes, and cattle. Being the son of a mechanic in a country town where pragmatism far outweighed book smart, I had to grow up quickly, and learn to not standout.

Primary school was easy and did not prepare me for highschool in the ways that matter. I attended a relatively poor state school for grades 1 to 7. I was an average white child in a school full of children from low socioeconomic backgrounds. My average backround actually put me in the 'top percentile' and set me up for a false sense of security. My parents decided that going to the local Catholic highschool was best for giving me a well rounded education and the best head start in life. Little did they know, sending me to the private highschool would only create insecurities and fuel an insatiable hunger for me to do better than those around me. Is this what my parents intended? Probably not, but here we are. I over-invest, insist I can do it all, and I do not give up easily making me incredibly dogmatic.

After highschool, I went to university. I originally started university at The University of Queensland in Brisbane, Queensland. I started studing pharmacy but after the first few weeks, I knew it wasnt for me. I think I moved to Brisbane to get away from my family. It was a tough time being gay and having no role models. I had to figure it out by myself without the help of anyone else. Day by day I was working it out. Reconciling my sexuality with what I was taught to be right and wrong. I had to redefine my entire belief system. Morals and ethics of a small town are often limited by small minds and do not encapsulate wordly views outside the narrow scope of tourists passing through town.

Then I started engineering at James Cook University in Townsville, Queensland. It was here that I met a really close friend. He was a different thinker. His ideas of what it meant to be a good person resonated with me. His approach to life was different -- refreshing. I owe a lot to Kevin. He was a springboard to the person I am today. Through university we worked to get each other across the line and graduated together. Today he is a control systems engineer and is succeeding in ways that so many people doubted that he ever would. He smashed the glass ceiling that his family built for him. I know they love him, but he needed room to rebel and grow beyond what they thought was possible.
